Thanksgiving Dinner For Two

Serves 2 with a few leftovers Total about 3.5 to 4 hours Hands on about 60 minutes

Lamb Shoulder Roast

Slow roasted lamb shoulder at a gentle heat so you can prep sides while it cooks.

Ingredients

  • 1 small bone in lamb shoulder, about 2 to 3 pounds
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, lightly crushed
  • 2 to 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 to 3 sprigs fresh thyme or oregano, optional
  • 1 small onion or a few shallots, roughly chopped, optional
  • 1 cup water or low sodium stock for the pan

Gravy From Lamb Drippings

  • Lamb roasting juices and browned bits from the pan
  • About 2 tablespoons lamb fat or butter
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 1.5 to 2 cups stock or water, as needed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Roast Vegetables

  • 1 to 1.5 pounds mixed roasting veg (carrots, parsnips, potatoes, Brussels sprouts, squash), cut into 1 to 1.5-inch pieces
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 to 1.5 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon chopped rosemary or thyme, optional
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced, optional
  • Finishing: lemon zest or a splash of vinegar, optional

Steps From First Coffee To Dinner

  1. Bring lamb to room temperature Take the lamb out of the fridge 30 to 45 minutes before cooking. Pat very dry with paper towels.
  2. Season the lamb Rub the lamb all over with salt and pepper, then drizzle with a little olive oil. Tuck garlic cloves and herb sprigs around the surface.
  3. Heat the oven Set the oven to 300 degrees Fahrenheit. Use a heavy oven safe pot or Dutch oven that has a lid.
  4. Sear the lamb Film the pot with olive oil and heat over medium high. Sear the lamb on all sides until nicely browned, about 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  5. Set up for slow roasting Add chopped onion if using and the garlic and herbs to the pot. Pour in the water or stock. The liquid should come a little way up the side, not cover the meat. Cover tightly with the lid or foil.
  6. Prep the vegetables for roasting Cut veg into even 1 to 1.5-inch pieces. Toss with olive oil, salt, pepper, and herbs on a large sheet pan and set aside. You’ll roast them at 425°F while the lamb rests.
  7. Slow roast the lamb Transfer to the oven and roast for about 3 to 4 hours, until the meat is very tender and pulls away easily with a fork. Check around the 2.5 hour mark and top up with a splash of water if the pan looks dry.
  8. Rest the lamb When tender, lift the lamb onto a warm plate, tent loosely with foil, and let it rest at least 20 to 30 minutes. Increase oven to 425°F for the vegetables.
  9. Roast the vegetables at 425°F Put the prepared veg sheet pan into the 425°F oven. Roast 20 to 25 minutes until browned and tender, tossing once halfway. Finish with a pinch of salt and lemon zest or a splash of vinegar if you like.
  10. Make the gravy While the vegetables roast, skim off most of the fat in the pot, leaving about 2 tablespoons and all the browned bits. Place the pot over medium heat, sprinkle in the flour, and whisk for 1 to 2 minutes.
  11. Thin and simmer the gravy Slowly whisk in 1.5 to 2 cups stock or water until smooth. Simmer 5 to 8 minutes until glossy and pourable, then taste and adjust salt and pepper.
  12. Carve and serve Pull the veg when browned and tender. Carve or pull the lamb, then serve with the roasted vegetables and plenty of gravy.
