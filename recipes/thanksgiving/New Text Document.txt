        <h2>Lamb Shoulder Roast</h2>
        <p class="note">
          Slow roasted lamb shoulder at a gentle heat so you can prep sides while it cooks.
        </p>
        <h3>Ingredients</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>1 small bone in lamb shoulder, about 2 to 3 pounds</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>2 teaspoons kosher salt</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>2 tablespoons olive oil</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>4 garlic cloves, lightly crushed</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>2 to 3 sprigs fresh rosemary</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>2 to 3 sprigs fresh thyme or oregano, optional</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>1 small onion or a few shallots, roughly chopped, optional</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>1 cup water or low sodium stock for the pan</div>
          </li>
        </ul>

        <h3>Gravy From Lamb Drippings</h3>
        <ul>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>Lamb roasting juices and browned bits from the pan</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>About 2 tablespoons lamb fat or butter</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>2 tablespoons flour</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>1.5 to 2 cups stock or water, as needed</div>
          </li>
          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>Salt and pepper to taste</div>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card steps">
        <h2>Steps From First Coffee To Dinner</h2>
        <ol id="steps">
          <li class="active">
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Bring lamb to room temperature</strong>
              <small>
                Take the lamb out of the fridge 30 to 45 minutes before cooking. Pat very dry with
                paper towels.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Season the lamb</strong>
              <small>
                Rub the lamb all over with salt and pepper, then drizzle with a little olive oil.
                Tuck garlic cloves and herb sprigs around the surface.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Heat the oven</strong>
              <small>
                Set the oven to 300 degrees Fahrenheit. Use a heavy oven safe pot or Dutch oven
                that has a lid.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Sear the lamb</strong>
              <small>
                Film the pot with olive oil and heat over medium high. Sear the lamb on all sides
                until nicely browned, about 3 to 4 minutes per side.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Set up for slow roasting</strong>
              <small>
                Add chopped onion if using and the garlic and herbs to the pot. Pour in the water
                or stock. The liquid should come a little way up the side, not cover the meat.
                Cover tightly with the lid or foil.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Slow roast the lamb</strong>
              <small>
                Transfer to the oven and roast for about 3 to 4 hours, until the meat is very
                tender and pulls away easily with a fork. Check around the 2.5 hour mark and top up
                with a splash of water if the pan looks dry.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Rest the lamb</strong>
              <small>
                When tender, lift the lamb onto a warm plate, tent loosely with foil, and let it
                rest at least 20 to 30 minutes while you make the gravy and finish the veg.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Make the gravy</strong>
              <small>
                Skim off most of the fat in the pot, leaving about 2 tablespoons and all the
                browned bits. Place the pot over medium heat, sprinkle in the flour, and whisk for
                1 to 2 minutes.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
            <input type="checkbox" />
            <div>
              <strong>Thin and simmer the gravy</strong>
              <small>
                Slowly whisk in 1.5 to 2 cups stock or water until smooth. Simmer 5 to 8 minutes
                until glossy and pourable, then taste and adjust salt and pepper.
              </small>
            </div>
          </li>

          <li>
