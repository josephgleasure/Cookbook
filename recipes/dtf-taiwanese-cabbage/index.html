Din Tai Fung Taiwanese Cabbage with Garlic

Side dish Total ~15 to 20 minutes Hands on ~10 minutes
Home

Ingredients

Quick high-heat stir-fry; keep ingredients ready.

Stir-Fry

  • 1 large head Taiwanese cabbage, cut into 2 to 3-inch pieces
  • 5 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup neutral oil (e.g., avocado, canola)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 teaspoon chicken bouillon powder (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper

Steps

  1. Prep all ingredients Cut cabbage into large bite-size pieces. Slice garlic thinly. Measure seasonings so theyâ€™re ready.
  2. Heat oil and bloom garlic Heat oil in a large wok over high heat until shimmering. Add garlic and stir 10 to 20 seconds until just fragrant (do not brown).
  3. Stir-fry cabbage with salt Add cabbage and salt. Stir-fry 3 to 4 minutes until it begins to wilt and release moisture.
  4. Reduce moisture; add Shaoxing wine Push cabbage to one side to let excess liquid evaporate briefly, then pour Shaoxing wine around the edge of the wok; let it sizzle and reduce.
  5. Season and finish Stir in chicken bouillon powder and white pepper. Cook 2 to 3 minutes more until tender but still crisp. Taste and adjust salt.
  6. Serve Plate immediately and serve hot as a side.
